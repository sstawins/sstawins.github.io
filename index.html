About me

Hello! I am a fourth year Astrophysics Ph.D candidate at University of California, Irvine interested in galaxy formation and evolution.
In 2019, I earned my MS in Astronomy at San Diego State University studying the gas around galaxies (specifically invovling Damped Lyman-alpha absorption line systems) and its effects on the host galaxy. Now at UCI, I have been working on detecting Lyman-alpha in emission at z > 4. Using the telescopes at Keck (mainly DEIMOS and LRIS), I have observed Lyman-alpha emission from distant galaxies. I am part of the WERLS collaboration looking for high-z Lyman-alpha emission in the EGS, COSMOS, and UDS fields using Keck/LRIS. I am also part of the MAGAZ3NE collaboration, specifically looking at detected Lyman-alpha emission from Ultra-Massive galaxies at z > 4.
Outside of my research, I believe mentorship, teaching, and outreach are incredibly important in encouraging others to pursue science and helping students succeed in academia. Throughout my career, I have worked to give back to the community in this way. I have volunteered in many outreach efforts and mentorship programs (read more about them here).
