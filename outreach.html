Organizations

Physics & Astronomy Community Excellence (PACE)

PACE is a graduate student led peer-mentoring program at UCI. The goal of the program is to help graduate students at all levels to be successful in grad school as well as teach our mentors valuable leadership and mentorship skills. In my second year at UCI, I joined the PACE leadership team and now am running the program with three other team members. Our programs include - Exceleration (1-on-1 grad peer mentoring for first-year graduates), Fire Up Fridays (bi-weekly group workshops for incoming grads), and Attitude Thrusters (group workshops for graduates at all levels). PACE is very thankful to be funded by AAS - National Osterbrock Leadership Program (NOLP).

The UCI Physics and Astronomy Blog

The UCI Physics and Astronomy Blog is a graduate student run organization that focuses on communication and outreach efforts within the Physics and Astronomy department at UCI. Our goal is to increase awareness of the department activities and research accomplishments, as well as communicate relevant physics topics through outreach. I have been on the team since my first year at UCI, and have multiple roles on the team. Mainly I have been in charge of our departments graduate student spotlight , which seeks to call attention to graduate students that have done extraordinary things within our department.

Physics Grad Cacus (PGC)

I am also an officer for the UCI Physics Grad Cacus, which is a graduate student-run organization within the Department of Physics and Astronomy that seeks to support and improve academic and career success, foster individual and community well-being, and be a voice for graduate students within our department.
